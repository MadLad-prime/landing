The Nunobium Manifesto

A fragmented blueprint for building things with glorious imperfection.

I. The Law of Opaque Causality

Code that works for reasons you don’t understand should not be questioned, merely revered. It is a gift from a past self who was clearly a genius. Do not touch it. Do not even look at it too hard.

[STATUS] SYSTEM: STABLE...ISH

II. The Schrödinger's Commit

A commit message is both descriptive and useless until the moment you need to `git blame`. "Fix stuff" is a perfectly valid incantation that appeases the Git gods in the short term.

[COORDINATES] X:42.007 Y:11.387 [VECTOR] 34.91° / 2.1 AU

III. The Documentation Paradox

Writing documentation is a love letter to your future self. Unfortunately, your future self suffers from amnesia and probably won't read it, assuming it's outdated anyway. Which it is.

IV. The Dependency Gambit

Why write ten lines of code when you can import a 2MB library to do it for you? The `node_modules` folder is not a burden; it is a monument to collaborative progress and collective technical debt.

V. The Principle of Belated Optimization

“Premature optimization is the root of all evil.” Therefore, the logical conclusion is to write the slowest possible code first, ensuring it becomes someone else's problem later. This is called job security.
